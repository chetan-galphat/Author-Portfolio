About

Shweta Sarthy is an Indian novelist whose work serves as a bridge between the silence of healing and the roar of self-discovery. Her writing doesn't just tell stories; it maps the emotional geography of women who have been tested by life and emerged as architects of their own fate.

From her roots in the heart of Madhya Pradesh to her current academic journey in Tamil Nadu, Shweta's evolution as a writer is deeply personal. Her transition from the depths of depression to a life anchored in self-love forms the visceral "heartbeat" of her prose. It is this lived experience that allows her to write with a "messy honesty" capturing the grit and grace of the human condition.

I believe a woman is the supreme power of the universe: delicate enough to feel every shadow, yet far too resilient to be broken. When she is nourished by love, she becomes a force that can conquer any world.

Following the lyrical exploration of Dread of Love and the poignant debut of Rose Beneath the Soil, her latest work, The Battle of Touch, stands as her most intimate project to date; an invitation to witness the ultimate strength found in vulnerability.
